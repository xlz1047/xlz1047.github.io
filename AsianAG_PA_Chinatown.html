Growing up as an Asian-American girl in Philadelphia, Chinatown held a special place in our hearts. It was more than just a neighborhood, it was our home where we felt a deep sense of belonging and comfort. Our identity is intertwined with Philadelphia's Chinatown, a treasured cultural gem that we hold dear. As we're constantly immersed in the sights, sounds, and smells of our community and culture, we cherish the priceless memories we made there.


We invite you to explore and embrace the vibrant community of Philadelphia's Chinatown and experience the same sense of connection and pride that we felt growing up. Let the sights, sounds, and smells guide you through the heart of our culture. May you create your treasured memories and strengthen the bonds we shared with this beloved neighborhood. So enjoy and explore!

The billionaire owners of the Philadelphia 76ers plan to build an arena next to Philadelphia's Chinatown, without consulting residents or community organizations. This threatens to disrupt the neighborhood's growth and vibrancy, and could negatively impact the lives of those who call Chinatown home. This is not the first time developers have made decisions for communities without their input, but residents have come together in the past to stop them. To protect Chinatown, residents are calling on others to join them in signing a petition to stop the arena's construction.

Visiting Philadelphia's Chinatown is a feast for the senses. The streets are alive with the colors and sounds of our culture, and the aromas of delicious food waft through the air. You can taste the richness of our heritage in the traditional dishes served in our restaurants, and experience the warmth of our community through our festivals and celebrations. From the colorful lanterns and dragon dances of the Lunar New Year, to the mooncakes and tea ceremonies of the Mid-Autumn Festival, each event offers a unique glimpse into our history and traditions. Our hope is that through exploring Chinatown's food, culture, and festival events, more people can come to appreciate the beauty and significance of our community.
